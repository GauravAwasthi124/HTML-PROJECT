Story of Lamgaundi

Lamgondi village is located in Ukhimath block of Rudraprayag district. The distance of this village is 8 km from Guptkashi. This village is known as the village of Kedarnath Teerth priests. Road, communication, electricity facilities are available in the village. Lamgondi village has about 175 families. This village also has Intermediate College, Post Office and Ayurvedic Hospital.

Lamgondi village is called the village of Kedarnath pilgrimage priests. Posti, Awasthi, Jugran, Vajpayee, Kurmanchali and Lal Moharia castes live here. Scheduled caste people also live in the village. An example of social harmony has been established in the village. Ramlila of this village is worth seeing in which women also participate. Ramlila is organized in the month of December after the doors of Kedarnath Dham are closed.

The old name of Lamgondi village is also Bamsu. The village worships Banasur, the grandson of King Bali and father of Usha, who is believed to be a descendant of Ravana. He is considered to be the deity of Asura nature who is the grandson of King Bali.Who was a great devotee of Lord Shiva. There was a big fight between King Banasura and Lord Krishna because of the love affair between Banasura's daughter Ukha and Aniruddha which led to a war between Banasura and Lord Krishna.

All the peaks of Kedar valley including Chaukhamba and Kedarnath mountain, Kedardom, Mandani Madmaheshwar are visible from Lamgondi village.
